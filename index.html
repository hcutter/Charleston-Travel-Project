Why Charleston?

Charleston, the Holy City

Charleston is known as the "Holy City" because of the many steeples and church spires that decorate its skyline. The city's long history of religious diversity and tolerance is reflected in its numerous churches, some of which date back to the 17th century. Charleston's churches have served as symbols of hope and resilience in times of hardship. Through their preservation and celebration, the city's religious heritage is still prominent today. Overall, the term "holy" is associated with Charleston due to its rich religious history and the enduring presence of its historic churches.
